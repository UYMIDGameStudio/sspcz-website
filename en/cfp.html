CALL FOR PAPERSTHE THIRD SESSION · 2026

Call for Papers

Submit your paper, share your thought.
This year's theme · "Change & the Unchanging"

The 3rd SSPCZ invites paper submissions from secondary school students passionate about philosophy. In each recommended direction, 2 "presenting papers" and 4 "attending papers" will be selected — presenting authors speak at the conference, and attending papers are published in this session's proceedings.

01 — SCOPE

Who & What

ELIGIBILITY
Secondary school students with a deep interest in philosophy who wish to present their research. Submissions must include both an abstract and the full paper for comprehensive review.
THEME
Change & the Unchanging

In a life-world of constant flux, what constitutes the "unchanging" we ought to hold — and how can that holding stay alive within change? Topics should engage the theme; all serious philosophical work is welcome. See the full committee letter on The 3rd Session page.

02 — REVIEW & AWARDS

Review & Awards

CRITERIA · 35 POINTS TOTAL
Argumentation (persuasiveness, logic, sound citation) — 15 pts
Thesis (fit with theme, scholarly value) — 15 pts
Originality (novelty) — 5 pts
AWARDS · FOUR PRIZES
Best Writing · Best Presentation
Best Theme · Best Originality
One author may win multiple awards
ORIGINALITY
Papers must be original, unpublished in any form and not under review elsewhere; they must pass academic-integrity screening, with authorship based on actual contribution.
AI POLICY
AI may not be an author or co-author; no more than 15% of the main text may be AI-generated; AI assistance must be disclosed in the acknowledgements, including tools and prompts.
* Detailed rules follow the official charter issued by the committee's academic team.
