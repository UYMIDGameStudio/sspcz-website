# RFC-200: Editorial Principles

## 1. Core Rule
Content correctness and academic rigor supersede visual aesthetics.

## 2. Naming & Typographical Conventions
- **Entity Naming**: The conference must be uniformly referred to as "SSPCZ" or the full bilingual equivalent. Themes must be capitalized consistently (e.g., "Change and Permanence").
- **Punctuation**: Strict adherence to bilingual punctuation rules. Do not mix half-width English punctuation with full-width Chinese characters unless explicitly required by citations.
- **Citations**: All philosophical quotes or references must follow a unified academic standard (APA or Chicago).

## 3. Archival Persistence
- **Permalinks**: URLs for past issues must remain permanently accessible and unchanged (e.g., `/archive/2025/cfp`).
- **Versioning**: When a new issue is published, the previous issue transitions to a read-only archive state but retains its exact visual and structural integrity.
