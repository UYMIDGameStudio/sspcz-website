The 3rd SSPCZ invites paper submissions from secondary school students passionate about philosophy. In each recommended direction, 2 "presenting papers" and 4 "attending papers" will be selected — presenting authors speak at the conference, and attending papers are published in this session's proceedings.

## Eligibility

Secondary school students with a deep interest in philosophy who wish to present their research. Submissions must include both an abstract and the full paper for comprehensive review.

## Theme

Topics should engage the theme "Change & Invariance": in a life-world of constant flux, what constitutes the invariant we ought to hold — and how can that holding stay alive within change? All serious philosophical work is welcome. The full exposition is in the committee's letter.

## Review Criteria (35 points total)

1. Argumentation (persuasiveness, logic, sound citation) — 15 points
2. Thesis (fit with theme, scholarly value) — 15 points
3. Originality (novelty) — 5 points

## Awards

Four prizes are conferred: Best Writing, Best Presentation, Best Theme and Best Originality. One author may win multiple awards.

## Originality

Papers must be original, unpublished in any form and not under review elsewhere; they must pass academic-integrity screening, with authorship based on actual contribution.

## AI Policy

AI may not be an author or co-author; no more than 15% of the main text may be AI-generated; AI assistance must be disclosed in the acknowledgements, including tools and prompts.

## How to Submit

For submissions and partnership, contact the committee by email with your abstract and full paper.

> Detailed rules follow the official charter issued by the committee's academic team; full review and integrity requirements are in the Conference Policies.
