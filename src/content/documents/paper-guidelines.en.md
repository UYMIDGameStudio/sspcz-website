This guide is for secondary school authors intending to submit to the SSPCZ. It covers a paper's topic, structure and academic requirements.

## Topic

Papers must engage the theme of the current session, pose a clear philosophical question and offer a serious argument. Original perspectives are encouraged; avoid broad surveys.

## Structure

A sound paper typically contains:

1. Abstract and keywords;
2. The question and its background;
3. The core argument (thesis, evidence, objections and replies);
4. Conclusion and remarks;
5. References.

## Citation

All citations must follow a unified academic standard (APA or Chicago), and must be self-consistent and verifiable. Cited philosophical texts must note edition and page.

## Academic Integrity

Papers must be original, with authorship based on actual contribution. AI use must follow the conference policy — see Conference Policies.
